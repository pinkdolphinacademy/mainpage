About Us

PinkDolphin is led by Mint, our founder and lead Behaviour Interventionist & Parent Coach. Services

What We Offer

Every service is built around one goal: meaningful, lasting progress — in the centre, at home, and in life.

🔍

Child Skill Assessment

Before anything begins, we take time to understand where your child is right now. Through careful observation we map out exactly what to build on — so every goal we set is the right one.

  • Communication and language — how your child requests, labels, and responds
  • Learning readiness — attention, imitation, following instructions
  • Social and play skills
  • Behaviour patterns — what triggers them and what maintains them
  • Daily living skills for older children working toward independence
  • Written summary of findings and recommended goals shared with the family
🎓

1:1 Behaviour Intervention

Individualised, structured sessions using evidence-based behaviour strategies — focused on the skills that matter most for your child's development right now.

  • Teaching your child to ask for what they want and need
  • Building vocabulary — naming objects, actions, and feelings
  • Answering questions and holding simple conversations
  • Reducing challenging or disruptive behaviours
  • Supporting skills to transfer naturally to school, home, and community
👨‍👩‍👧

Parent Coaching Our Speciality

Our structured coaching system empowers caregivers to become confident, consistent partners in their child's development — beyond the therapy room.

  • 3-part coaching system: goals → strategies → home practice
  • Live modelling and guided practice each session
  • 1–2 home goals per week — practical, not overwhelming
  • Behaviour management strategies for daily routines
  • Weekly review and program adjustment

Why It Works

Parent Coaching Is Our Core Differentiator

Many providers focus only on what happens inside a session. We focus on what happens everywhere else — because that is where real life takes place. You leave every session knowing exactly what to do and why.

Every coaching session follows this format:

1
Review — What happened at home this week? What worked, what didn't? (5–10 min)
2
Model — We demonstrate the skill or strategy clearly, step by step
3
Parent practises — You try it with your child, with us coaching you live
4
Feedback & adjust — We refine it together until it feels natural and comfortable
5
1–2 home goals only — Specific, realistic, and immediately useful this week
🎯

Part A — Assessment → Goal System

Through structured observation, we identify 3–5 priority goals per child: one language goal, one learning readiness goal, and one behaviour goal. Focused and realistic for parents to work on.

📋

Part B — Program + Strategy System

Each goal becomes a clear, parent-friendly plan: what to say, when to help, and what to do when your child gets it right. Simple enough to use at breakfast or at bedtime.

🏠

Part C — Parent Coaching System

We coach caregivers to be the most consistent support their child has. Weekly updates keep goals progressing and difficulty increasing as your child grows.

FAQ

Common Questions

What is a Behaviour Interventionist?

A Behaviour Interventionist works with children to build skills and reduce challenging behaviours using structured, evidence-based strategies. Our work focuses specifically on communication, learning readiness, and behaviour — guided by careful observation and personalised goal planning.

What are your qualifications?

Our lead practitioner, Mint, has several years of hands-on experience as a Senior ABA Therapist working with children with diverse learning and behavioural needs. We apply evidence-based behaviour strategies and use structured observation to guide our work. We are not clinical psychologists or licensed therapists — our scope is implementation, coaching, and guidance, not diagnosis.

What ages do you work with?

Primarily young children aged 2–8, where early support has the greatest impact. We also work with older children who are still building foundational communication and daily living skills — developmental level matters more than age. We discuss the right fit together in the free consultation.

Does my child need a diagnosis to start?

Not necessarily. While many children we support have an autism or developmental delay diagnosis, ABA strategies benefit any child with communication delays, learning difficulties, or behavioural challenges. We discuss each child's individual needs in the free consultation first.

What does a typical session look like?

Sessions are structured but warm and playful — not clinical. We use your child's interests and motivation to make learning feel natural. Progress is recorded every session so we always have a clear, honest picture of how your child is developing.

What makes your parent coaching different?

Most providers coach parents by handing over a handout. We coach live — you practise strategies in real time with us beside you, receive immediate feedback, and leave with 1–2 specific, achievable home goals. You become your child's most consistent and confident supporter.

How soon will we see progress?

Most families notice meaningful changes within 4–8 weeks when therapy sessions and home coaching are consistent. Progress depends on session frequency and caregiver follow-through — which is exactly why our parent coaching system is built into everything we do.

Can we do parent coaching without the 1:1 sessions?

Yes. Parent coaching is available as a standalone service. Many families find it valuable to start here — building practical strategies before or alongside direct therapy. It is also a strong option if your child is already receiving therapy elsewhere and you want consistent home support.
